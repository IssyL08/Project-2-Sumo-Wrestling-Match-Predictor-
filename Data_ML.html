Final Result

**Understanding & Preparing Data:**

  • Headers with Japanese terms were translated to English (stable for heya, wrestler for rikishi, tournament for basho, etc.).
  • All divisions below Makuuchi were dropped.
  • All non-active wrestlers were dropped.
  • Ranks were given a numerical value (from greatest to lowest: Yokozuna, Ozeki, Sekiwake,Komusubi, M1-18).
  • Wrestler's age at the time of the tournament was calculated.
  • For all matches, the differences in height, weight, age, and rank were calculated.

    • **Machine Learning**

  • Click hyperlink below to access Google Colab Notebook:
    • Google Colab Notebook For Machine Learning Info
  • Features used for machine learning: differences in height, weight, age and rank.
  • ML methods used: Logistic Regression, Decision Tree Classifier, Random Forest
  • Logistic Regression gave the highest Training/Testing Scores (59/55% respectively)
  • GridSearchCV was attempted but it took TOO DARN LONG!!!
  • The LR model was trained using data from May 2004 to March 2021 and tested with May 2021 tournament results.
  • The model came close to some actual results! It predicted Takakeisho as the winner (he was runner-up) while the actual winner, Terunofuji was predicted as the runner-up. Not bad!
  • Ichinojo was the only perfectly predicted outcome with 9 wins, while the model predictions for Terunofuji, Wakatakakage and Shimanoumi were off by just 1 win.
  • Things to improve the model: add more features like match win percentages or wrestler injuries.
  • Sumo has a very subjective nature to it, how wrestlers move up and down ranks can vary wildly from one tournament to another, and rules are not exactly written in stone.
